Admission Requirements

  1. a.1 Students shall be accepted regardless of age, sex, nationality, religious beliefs, socio-economic status, or political affiliations.
  2. a.2. Students must have graduated from government recognized secondary schools/Alternative Learning System.
  3. a.3. Students must meet all the following admission requirements:
  4. i. DMMMSU College Admission Test
  5. ii. Form 138 or ALS Rating
  6. iii. Certificate of Good Moral Character
  7. iv. Three (3) pcs. 2" x 2" and 1 pc. 1" x 1" colored pictures with white background
  8. v. Copy of Birth Certificate from the National Statistics Office
  9. vi. Qualify in the interview
  10. vii. Qualify in the physical/medical examination conducted by DMMMSU or any government physician.

    • Application Process

    1. Please use the online enrollment process below as a reference:
    2. 1. Online Registration
    3. Qualified New Students
    4. Before completing the online registration system, download, print and fill out the Free Higher Education (FHE) Form. Prepare a scanned copy of your PSA birth certificate, Senior High School Card and 2X2 picture with a name tag (you may use your smart phones to capture these requirements if there are no available scanners).
    5. Wait for your code numbers to be sent to you by your program chairperson via text, call, or email. This will serve as your password to access our Online Registration System. The link of the Online Registration will be uploaded soon.
    6. Old Students
    7. Download, print,fill out, and scan (you may use your smart phones to capture these requirements if there are no available scanners) the FHE form.
    8. Use your last name as your username and your ID Number as your password.
    9. For new students and old and regular students, you are not required to undertake the succeeding steps. The designated faculty member in your college will do it for you. Wait for the confirmation of your enrollment.
    10. 2. Subject Loading
    11. The chairperson will encode your subjects into the enrollment system.
    12. 3. Enlistment and Assessment
    13. The in-charge faculty member will enlist and assess your subjects in the system.
    14. For irregular (old) students, visit your respective colleges during the scheduled date of enrollment to personally identify and choose your preferred subjects to be enrolled in.
    15. 4. Payment
    16. For new students, no fees (tuition fee and miscellaneous fees) will be collected as long as you have availed of the Free Higher Education Scholarship. However, if there are fees (mostly for old students) which are not included in the FHE, the student must settle their account balances via our campus remittance partner or campus Landbank account or at the cashiers office observing minimum health standards before proceeding to the next step.
    17. 5. Validation
    18. The registrars office will validate your assessment certificates as officially enrolled.
    19. 6. ID Application
    20. New students and transferees will proceed to the MIS Office to apply for a school ID. The schedule of ID applications will be announced during the first face-to-face meeting.
    21. 7. Student Portal
    22. For new students and transferees, proceed to the library or other identified locations to register for their student portal. The schedule of Student Portal Enrollment will be announced during the first face-to-face meeting.
